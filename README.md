Vegetation monitoring using UAV and Lidar
Module 2 – Unmanaged Laser Scanning (ULS) point clouds
Practical Work
Estimating forest inventory variables using unmanaged Laser Scanning (ULS) point clouds
General Rules:
●	Groups: The work must be carried out in groups of 3-4 students. Groups should be formed during the first class of the module (11/03/2026).
●	Evaluation: The assignment will account for 40% of the final grade, assessed based on the submission of a document (100%) 
Submission Format:
●	The work must be presented in pdf format.
●	The objective is to present visually and concisely: 1) Generate basic products from a LiDAR-drone point cloud, including a digital terrain model (DTM) and a canopy height model (CHM) 2) Estimates of variables of interest for forest management (Wood Volume (VCC) and Aboveground biomass (AGB)) at both the stand level and the individual tree level and 3) Compare the results in terms of Wood Volume (VCC) and Aboveground biomass (AGB) at the pixel level and crown level
●	Different LiDAR-drone point clouds will be assigned for each group in order to apply the methods you learnt during the practical classes.
Deadlines:
●	The assignment must be submitted by 18/05/2026 in PDF format on the platform onedrive (Course_UAV_LiDAR_ISA_2026) or Fenix course.
●	Students must also attach the R script used to perform the analysis.
Work Structure
1.	Identification – Title, group members, course and ISA identification.
2.	Introduction – Presentation of objectives of the work. 
3.	Methods – Summary description of the methodology applied, detailing the steps taken in the analysis.
4.	Graphical Presentation of Results
5.	Concise Discussion of the Results
6.	Sources: refer any data source, method or document used to realize the work.
